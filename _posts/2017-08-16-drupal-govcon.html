Drupal GovCon is an annual convention that brings together people from all over the country who use, develop, design, and support the Drupal platform with a focus in government. We wanted to share with you some of our favorite sessions from this year’s Con.

Back up — what is Drupal?

“Droo pull”
Drupal is an open source content management framework — it provides a back-end framework for at least 2.3% of all websites worldwide, including...

  • Companies like CVS Health, General Electric, Time Warner, and Whole Foods Market
  • Nonprofit orgs like Amnesty International, Oxfam International, The Hunger Project, and WETA
  • Government agencies in over 150 countries

Smorgasbord of Sessions

Here are some of our faves from this year's Con:

  • Making mass.gov data-driven and constituent-centric
    A look at how Massachusetts is redesigning and reorganizing their government website — check out their pilot site.
  • Accessible websites can’t be achieved by just one person. Learn how to get your whole team involved
    What your team should keep in mind when designing, writing, and developing an accessible site, and some tools you can use to test if your site is accessible.
  • Content migrations don’t have to be a nightmare
    Tips for managing client expectations (because we all need those) and internal processes for migrating sites over to Drupal.
